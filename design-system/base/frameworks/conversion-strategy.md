# Conversion Strategy Framework
The definitive guide for defining the brand narrative and tactical goals of a website.
---
## 1. Goal Alignment
Every strategy starts by selecting a "North Star" goal.
| Goal | Primary Weight | Strategy Focus |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| **Lead Generation** | Conversion | Trust signals, friction removal, outcome-focused copy. |
| **Brand Awareness** | Perception | Storytelling, unique visuals, depth of mission. |
| **Portfolio/Studio** | Proof | Quality of work, voice/personality, process transparency. |
| **E-commerce** | Transaction | Product utility, social validation, "Add to Cart" velocity. |
| **Community** | Belonging | FOMO, shared values, clear entry points. |
---
## 2. Narrative Strategy (StoryBrand)
*   **The Hero:** Position the customer as the Hero and the brand as the **Guide**.
*   **The Problem:** Identify the Hero's specific desire and agitate the **Internal Frustration** (how the problem makes them feel).
*   **The Guide's Attributes:**
    *   **Empathy:** "We get you."
    *   **Authority:** "We have the credentials/results." (Impact Numbers, Logo Grids, Certifications).
*   **The Plan:** Provide a clear 3-step plan to remove risk.
*   **The Stakes:** Explicitly state the Success (Life post-solution) and the Failure (The cost of staying stuck).
---
## 3. Positioning & CTA Strategy
*   **Positioning:** [Brand] is the [Category] that [Primary Benefit] for [Audience] who [Pain Point].
*   **The "One Job":** The single most important action the homepage drives (Primary CTA).
*   **CTA Strategy:**
    *   **Primary:** High-commitment action (Book Now, Buy, Hire).
    *   **Transitional (Lead Gen):** Low-commitment for those not ready (Guide, PDF, Newsletter).
---
## 4. Writing & UX Standards
*   **Above the Fold:** Within 5 seconds, users must know: What you offer, how it helps, and how to start.
*   **Authority Band:** Immediately below the hero. Use either a **Logo Grid** (3rd party trust) or **Impact Numbers** (Internal credibility e.g., "500+ Projects Completed", "98% Satisfaction").
*   **Cognitive Load:** Progressive disclosure. punchy, scannable copy. No "Happy Talk" intro fluff.
*   **Continuity:** Branding must match the user's entry point (ad/link).
