#### Section 3 — Problem/Solution
**Type:** Side-by-Side (or full-width alternating blocks)
**Purpose:** Agitate the GCC product leader's specific friction, then reframe with the approach.

**Block A — The Problem (left/top):**
> **Headline:** "You have the team. You have the mandate. But every meeting ends the same way."
>
> **Body:** Your roadmap has more opinions than decisions. Your stakeholders want different things. Your team is capable, but they're building in circles. You know something needs to shift — you're just not sure what, or how fast you can move.

**Block B — The Solution (right/bottom):**
> **Headline:** "A Design Sprint doesn't just produce output. It produces clarity."
>
> **Body:** In five focused days, I'll take your team from "we think we know" to "we know, and here's the evidence." No six-week research cycles. No slide decks full of recommendations. A real prototype, tested with real users, and a real decision — made together.

**Copy Notes:**
- "You" and "your" should dominate this section. Sanjivanee is not the subject.
- The word "clarity" is a key emotional unlock for this audience.

#### Section 5 — Process
**Type:** Numbered Steps (horizontal or waterfall scroll)
**Purpose:** Remove risk by making the path to working together clear and low-friction.

| Step | Name | Body |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Discovery Call (30 min) | We talk about your challenge, your team, and your timeline. No sales pitch — just diagnosis. I'll tell you honestly whether a sprint is the right move. |
| **2** | Sprint Design | I map the sprint challenge, design the five-day agenda, and brief your team. You show up ready. I handle the preparation. |
| **3** | Sprint Week | Five days of focused work: understand, sketch, decide, prototype, test. Your team participates. Your stakeholders see the output. A real decision gets made. |
| **4** | Follow-Through | Post-sprint debrief, findings documentation, and a clear next-step recommendation. Some teams go straight into the next sprint. Some need space to absorb and execute. Both are fine. |

**Visual Direction:** Clean numbered sequence, generous spacing, single accent color on the step numbers. Not a busy flowchart.

## Copy Architecture Rules (Applies Across All Pages)

1. **Above the fold:** Within 5 seconds, visitors know: *What she does, who it's for, and how to start.*
2. **No "Happy Talk":** No "Welcome to my website," no "I'm passionate about design," no fluffy openers.
3. **GCC specificity:** The audience is named in the hero and at least two other sections.
4. **Outcome framing:** Every service description ends with the result, not the deliverable.
5. **One Job per section:** Each section has one question it answers and one action it supports.
6. **CTA frequency:** Primary CTA appears in: Hero, Close section, and at least one mid-page section.
